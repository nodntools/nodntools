North America Tipping Guide

Tipping Etiquette: How Much to Tip in North America?

Tipping is a normal part of dining, travel, and everyday service culture across the United States and Canada. If you are wondering how much to tip, a helpful starting point is to look at the type of service, the quality of the experience, and whether any service charge has already been added to the bill.

For many sit-down restaurants, the standard tip for restaurant service is often around 15% to 20% before tax, with 18% commonly used as a middle-ground option. Some diners tip more for excellent service, large groups, or special requests, while a lower tip may be used when service was limited or below expectations.

Understanding tipping etiquette in North America can make travel and daily life feel much smoother. At restaurants, cafés, bars, hair salons, taxis, rideshare services, hotels, and delivery services, tipping customs can vary. A quick coffee may only involve a small optional tip, while a full-service dinner, haircut, or taxi ride may involve a percentage-based tip.

When dining as a group, splitting the bill with friends is easier when everyone agrees on the tip percentage before paying. If one person pays the full check, this calculator can help estimate the total bill with tip and the amount per person. For a smoother experience, confirm whether the group wants to split evenly or pay based on individual orders.

It is also important to understand gratuity vs tip. A tip is usually an optional amount added by the customer, while gratuity may refer to a service charge that is automatically included on the receipt, especially for large parties, banquets, cruises, or certain tourist areas. If automatic gratuity is already included, you may not need to add another full tip unless you want to reward exceptional service.
