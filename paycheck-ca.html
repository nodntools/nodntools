Understanding Your Canadian Paycheck Breakdown

Navigating your gross earnings versus your actual net take-home pay can be complex in Canada. When you receive your salary, several mandatory federal and provincial deductions are made automatically at source. Here is a breakdown of what impacts your Canadian paycheck in 2026.

1. Federal Income Tax

Federal income tax is a progressive tax levied by the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA). Canada uses progressive tax brackets depending on your taxable income level. The calculations also integrate the Basic Personal Amount (BPA) tax credit, ensuring lower-income tranches remain protected from immediate taxation.

2. Provincial Income Tax

Depending on your province of employment, independent provincial tax schedules apply. Provinces like British Columbia (BC) and Ontario (ON) utilize separate progressive structures, meaning your geographical location directly adjusts your final aggregate tax rate.

3. CPP and EI Statutory Deductions

Two critical national insurance funds are deducted automatically up to strict statutory annual limits:

  • CPP (Canada Pension Plan): Deducted at a base rate with a secondary tier (CPP2) applying to higher income tranches up to annual maximum thresholds.
  • EI (Employment Insurance): Deducted at a flat percentage rate up to the maximum annualized insurable earnings cap.
