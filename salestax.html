Why US Sales Tax Varies by State

Sales tax in the United States is not set by one single national rate. Instead, it is usually determined at the state level and may also include local county, city, district, or special-purpose tax rates. This is why the same item can cost a different final amount depending on where it is purchased or shipped.

Some states, such as Oregon, Delaware, Montana, New Hampshire, and Alaska, are commonly known for having no statewide general sales tax. Other states, such as California, Texas, Florida, Washington, Illinois, and New York, apply state sales tax rates that may be combined with additional local taxes.

Online shoppers should also remember that sales tax may be calculated based on the shipping destination, marketplace rules, seller tax nexus, and product category. For example, clothing, groceries, digital products, and services may be taxed differently depending on the state.
