Gross Pay vs. Net Pay

Gross pay is your salary before taxes and deductions. Net pay, also called take-home pay, is the amount you actually receive after estimated taxes and withholding. A paycheck calculator helps bridge the gap between a salary offer and the monthly income you can use for rent, mortgage payments, groceries, savings, debt repayment, transportation, childcare, and everyday expenses.

Federal Income Tax Estimate

Federal income tax is progressive, which means higher portions of taxable income are taxed at higher rates. This calculator estimates federal tax after applying a simplified 2026 standard deduction for a single filer. Your actual withholding may differ depending on Form W-4 settings, dependents, credits, pre-tax deductions, additional withholding, and filing status.

State Income Tax Estimate

State income tax varies widely across the United States. Some states have no wage income tax, while others use flat or progressive tax systems. This calculator uses simplified estimated state rates so users can compare how location affects monthly take-home pay. Exact state withholding may depend on deductions, credits, local tax rules, and employer payroll settings.

Effective Tax Rate

The effective tax rate is the estimated total tax divided by annual salary. It is different from your top marginal tax bracket. Understanding effective tax rate can make it easier to compare job offers, relocation options, salary increases, bonuses, and income changes because it shows the overall percentage of gross pay going toward estimated income taxes.

What May Affect Your Real Paycheck

Actual paycheck amounts can vary because payroll calculations may include Social Security, Medicare, local income tax, retirement contributions, health insurance premiums, dental and vision plans, flexible spending accounts, health savings accounts, commuter benefits, union dues, wage garnishments, bonuses, overtime, commissions, and employer-specific deductions. This calculator provides a simplified income tax estimate and should be used as a planning tool rather than an exact payroll statement.

Your Form W-4 also matters. If you claim dependents, request extra withholding, have multiple jobs, receive investment income, or qualify for tax credits, your actual federal withholding may differ from a simple salary estimate. For accurate paycheck planning, compare this estimate with your paystub, employer payroll portal, IRS resources, and professional tax guidance.

Tips for Understanding Take-Home Pay

When evaluating a salary, focus on both gross income and net income. A higher salary may not always translate into the same increase in monthly take-home pay after taxes and deductions. Consider the full compensation package, including retirement match, health benefits, paid time off, bonuses, equity, commuting costs, remote work flexibility, state taxes, and cost of living. A paycheck calculator helps you ask better questions before making a career or relocation decision.

US Paycheck Calculator FAQ

What is take-home pay?

Take-home pay is the estimated amount of income left after taxes and deductions. It is also called net pay or after-tax income.
